Welcome! I'm Aida ("eye-duh").
I work at the intersection of exoplanets and galactic archaeology.

... Which exists now that we've entered the era of large-scale surveys, and are discovering exoplanets on industrial-level scales - it's an exciting time!

I use ML and other statistical techniques to extract information (e.g., kinematics, chemical abundances, ages) on stars and their planets from big survey (e.g., Gaia, SDSS) datasets, and targeted multi-wavelength spectroscopic/radial velocity observations. I'm interested in:

  1. Planet demographics throughout different regions of our Galaxy, the Milky Way
    (i.e, thin/thick disk, halo)
  2. Signatures of planetary system formation and dynamics (e.g., planet engulfment) embedded in host star chemistry
  3. Chemical (in)homogeneity of co-moving stars as tracers of the Milky Way's formation history

I grew up in Salem, Oregon. I was an undergrad at Yale (B.S., Physics, 2015), a graduate student at Caltech (Ph.D., Planetary Science, 2023), and a Kalbfleisch Postdoctoral Fellow at the American Museum of Natural History (AMNH) (2023-2024) before starting my current position.

This is my cat, Interstellar Medium ("Izzy").
